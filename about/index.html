Our Story

From a small backyard grill to a downtown institution — the Flames story.

Est. 2010

Born from a Passion for Fire

Flames began in 2010 when chef Marcus Reyne converted an old warehouse on Ember Lane into what he called "a temple to fire." His philosophy was simple: real wood fire, seasonal produce, no shortcuts.

In the first year, Flames was a 20-seat neighbourhood spot with a handwritten menu that changed daily. Word spread quickly. By 2013, we had expanded to 80 seats and earned our first regional recognition from the Culinary Herald.

Today, Flames seats over 120 guests across two dining rooms and a private event space, while staying true to the original vision: honest cooking, bold fire, and a room that feels alive.

Our Values

🌿

Local & Seasonal

We source from farms within 50 miles. Our menu changes with the seasons because the best ingredient is always the freshest one.

🔥

Fire First

Wood fire is the oldest and most honest cooking method. We use hickory, cherry, and oak — chosen for the dish, not for convenience.

🤝

Community Roots

We employ locally, train from within, and give back to the neighbourhood that gave us our start. Flames is a community project.

Through the Years

Our Timeline

2010
Flames opens with 20 seats, a wood-fire grill, and a daily handwritten menu on Ember Lane.
2013
First expansion to 80 seats. Named "Best New Restaurant" by Culinary Herald. Stefan Adler joins as sommelier.
2016
Private dining room opens. Flames enters Top 50 Restaurants regional list for the first time.
2019
Isabelle Chen joins as Head Pastry Chef. The dessert menu earns a separate dedicated feature in Gastronome magazine.
2022
Full renovation of the main dining room. New Josper oven installed. Tasting menu launched Fridays & Saturdays.
2026
Flames marks 16 years. Now serving 120 guests nightly, with the same fire, the same passion, the same Ember Lane address.
